Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: December 1, 2025

Our Commitment

Basic ASL App is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

Conformance Status

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines help make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and more user-friendly for everyone.

Accessibility Features

Our app includes the following accessibility features:

  • Clear Visual Design: High contrast text and simple, clean layouts for better readability
  • Keyboard Navigation: Full keyboard support for users who cannot use a mouse
  • Screen Reader Support: Properly labeled elements and ARIA attributes for assistive technologies
  • Adjustable Text: Content that can be resized without breaking functionality
  • Visual Learning: Video demonstrations with clear visual cues for ASL signs
  • Alternative Formats: Multiple ways to access learning content
  • PoseNet Integration: AI-powered pose detection that provides real-time feedback on sign accuracy, helping users learn proper hand positions and movements without audio cues

PoseNet Accessibility

Our PoseNet feature enhances learning accessibility through:

  • Real-Time Visual Feedback: Instant visual confirmation when signs are performed correctly
  • Motion Tracking: Camera-based pose detection that analyzes hand and body positions
  • Privacy-First: All processing happens on your device - no images are stored or transmitted
  • Progress Indicators: Visual cues showing accuracy scores and improvement areas
  • Self-Paced Learning: Practice at your own speed with immediate feedback
  • No Audio Required: Completely visual learning experience ideal for Deaf and hard of hearing users
  • Adjustable Sensitivity: Customize detection sensitivity based on your skill level

For Deaf and Hard of Hearing Users

As an ASL learning app, we recognize the importance of serving the Deaf community:

  • All audio content includes visual alternatives
  • Video content features clear, well-lit sign demonstrations
  • Written descriptions accompany visual content
  • Multiple camera angles for complex signs (where available)
  • Cultural context and notes about ASL usage

For Users with Visual Impairments

We strive to make our content accessible to users with visual impairments:

  • Descriptive alt text for all images and icons
  • Text descriptions of ASL signs and movements
  • Compatible with screen readers and magnification software
  • Clear color contrast ratios throughout the app

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some limitations may exist:

  • Some third-party content may not be fully accessible
  • Older devices may have limited feature support
  • Complex interactive elements may require updates for full accessibility

We are actively working to address these limitations and improve accessibility continuously.

Feedback

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of Basic ASL App. If you encounter any accessibility barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please let us know:

mail accessibility@basicasl.com

We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 3-5 business days.

Technical Specifications

Basic ASL App's accessibility relies on the following technologies:

  • HTML5
  • WAI-ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications)
  • CSS3
  • JavaScript

Assessment Approach

Basic ASL App has been assessed using the following methods:

  • Internal accessibility audits
  • Automated testing tools
  • User testing with assistive technologies
  • Feedback from the Deaf and disability communities
